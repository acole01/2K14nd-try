Customized Drive Icons
Summer 2011
Customization has always been a hobby of mine, from past LJ layouts to my DS homebrew skin. After completing a series of character designs for my external storage, the next logical step seemed to be to add the art onto the drives somehow.

Some devices come with customized icons/logos pre-installed that autorun when the device is plugged in--that seemed like a really neat way to "attach" the art, and for one thing it would be more durable than applying the art physically to the drive covers. But how to do it...?

After some brief research, I'd learned that Windows requires four different sizes for icons at 16, 32, 48, and 256 pixels squared, found a program capable of converting image formats into .ico, standard icon format, and also that getting the icons to appear on plug-in was as simple as a one line autorun file.

Story Sheets
Summer 2012

Over one summer I set out to finally produce something towards my many personal narratives that I could put out there and be strong enough stand alone, while also referencing/stoking interest for the larger stories. Obsessive overtasker that I am, this demanded I create icons for all 32 of my characters—but with revisions included (I am a perfectionist) I ended up making 48 icons in total. It was a lot of work, but the enjoyable stimulating kind.

Presenting them all as kind of dingy photocopies from a poorly translated guide was my original intention: it rang true to my aesthetic, that of my projects, and my sense of humor, but with plotlines already parsed down to blurbs, the further stress of engrish was too much to faithfully convey the needed info.

It was fun to immitate the analog artifacts of poor photocopies completely digitally,and I definitely plan to revisit the aesthetic.

Identify Yourself

Personal branding.

current: hex the icosahedron has become a prominent motif in my works, featured in patterns to narrative pieces, functioning as a kind of identifier for myself. Aside from the personal significance it holds, graphically strong too; its silhouette simplifies down to a hexagon, easily recognizable, more distinct than a circle or rectangle while neutrality of a basic shape. Its neutrality wouldnt distract from the image when using as a watermark/stamp and can be dressed up as several variations show. these factors led me to use in my personal logomark.
